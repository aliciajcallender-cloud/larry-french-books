About the Author

Four Decades of Walking Families Toward Healing

M.A. Marriage, Family & Child Counseling Licensed Professional Counselor (LPC) Licensed Marriage & Family Therapist (LMFT) Gottman Couples Therapy — Level III Focus on the Family Referral Resource 43-Year AAMFT Member

Larry L. French is a Licensed Professional Counselor and Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist with a Master of Arts in Marriage, Family and Child Counseling from Azusa Pacific University. He is the founder and Executive Director of the Virginia Center for Family Relations — a Christian counseling practice dedicated to helping individuals, couples, and families find healing rooted in both clinical excellence and faith.

Having completed all three levels of the Gottman Training for Marriage Counseling, Larry brings research-backed couples therapy approaches alongside his deep grounding in Family Systems and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy. He has served as a Clinical Supervisor with the AAMFT, a Counselor Supervisor with the Virginia Board of Professional Counselors, and a Field Practicum Supervisor with the University of Virginia. He is a trusted referral resource for Focus on the Family and a long-standing member of the American Association of Christian Counselors.

Before entering the counseling profession, Larry served four years in the United States Coast Guard — including search and rescue flights out of Miami. That grounding in service, courage, and human care has shaped everything he does as a counselor, supervisor, and now as an author.

"My deepest desire is to provide the finest psychological care that flows from a Christian world and life view — to help facilitate healing for the emotional and relational hurts of individuals and families in our community."
